How Does Prime Performance Work?

  1. Luteinizing Hormone (LH) Stimulation: Ingredients like D-Aspartic Acid Calcium Chelate signal the pituitary gland to release more LH, which in turn triggers testosterone production in the testes at the Leydig cell level.
  2. SHBG Reduction: Stinging Nettle Root extract binds to SHBG proteins in the blood, freeing up bound testosterone and making more of it biologically active and usable by tissues and receptors.

Testosterone Co-Factor

Zinc is one of the most well-researched minerals for male hormonal health. It functions as a co-factor in over 300 enzymatic reactions, including those directly involved in testosterone biosynthesis. Clinical evidence indicates that zinc deficiency is strongly correlated with reduced testosterone levels, and supplementation reliably restores normal hormonal function in deficient men.

Vitamin D3
Hormone Precursor Support

Vitamin D3 functions as a steroid hormone precursor and has specific receptor sites on testosterone-producing Leydig cells. Studies show men with optimal vitamin D levels consistently have higher testosterone readings. A 12-month clinical study reported a 25 percent increase in testosterone in men supplementing with 3,332 IU of D3 daily compared to placebo.

Stinging Nettle Root Extract
SHBG Reduction

SHBG (sex hormone-binding globulin) binds to free testosterone and renders it unavailable for use by cells and tissues. Stinging nettle root contains compounds called lignans that bind competitively to SHBG, freeing up bound testosterone. This mechanism is particularly relevant for men over 40, in whom SHBG levels naturally increase.

Luteolin
Estrogen Management

Luteolin is a plant-derived flavonoid that acts as a natural aromatase inhibitor, reducing the enzyme-driven conversion of testosterone into estrogen. For aging men, this is a critical mechanism because elevated estrogen not only reduces testosterone but also contributes to unwanted body fat distribution. Research suggests luteolin has selective inhibitory effects without the side effects of synthetic estrogen blockers.

Mucuna Pruriens Extract
Dopamine and LH Support

Mucuna pruriens is standardized for L-DOPA content, a direct precursor to dopamine. Elevated dopamine signaling stimulates LH release, thereby indirectly boosting testosterone production. Research suggests that mucuna pruriens supplementation also improves sperm quality and reduces cortisol, making it a dual-action ingredient in this formula.
